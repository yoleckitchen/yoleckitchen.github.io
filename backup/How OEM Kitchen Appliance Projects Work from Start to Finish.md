A clear market direction helps the factory recommend the right solution.

# Step 3: Product Discussion and Engineering Review

After selecting a factory, the project usually enters the product development stage.

This is where buyers discuss details with the manufacturer.

Important topics include:

## Product Design

- Product appearance
- Materials
- Colors
- Structure

## Product Function

- Heating performance
- Temperature control
- Safety features
- User experience

## Market Requirements

- Voltage
- Plug type
- Certification needs
- Packaging requirements

A professional factory does not simply ask:

"How many pieces do you need?"

They also ask:

"How will your customers use this product?"

# Step 4: Quotation and Project Evaluation

After understanding the requirements, the factory provides a quotation.

A complete OEM quotation usually includes:

- Unit price
- MOQ
- Sample cost
- Tooling cost (if required)
- Packaging cost
- Production lead time

Many buyers only compare prices.

However, experienced importers evaluate the complete project cost.

A cheaper quotation may create additional costs later because of:

- Quality issues
- Delayed production
- Product modifications
- Communication problems

The right supplier is not always the cheapest one.

---

# Step 5: Prototype and Sample Development

Before mass production, the factory creates samples.

This is one of the most important stages in OEM manufacturing.

The sample allows buyers to check:

## Appearance

- Design
- Color
- Surface finishing
- Materials

## Performance

- Product function
- User experience
- Safety

## Production Feasibility

- Can the product be manufactured consistently?
- Are there production difficulties?
- Can costs be controlled?

A good factory uses the sample stage to improve the product, not just to show a prototype.

---

# Step 6: Testing and Certification Preparation

Kitchen appliances involve electricity, heating systems, and sometimes food contact materials.

Therefore, certification planning should happen early.

Depending on the target market, products may require:

- CE
- CB
- UKCA
- ETL
- RoHS
- LFGB

A professional OEM supplier should understand these requirements before production begins.

This helps avoid problems such as:

- Failed testing
- Production delays
- Additional modification costs

# OEM vs ODM: Understanding the Difference

Many buyers are unsure whether they need OEM or ODM.

## OEM Manufacturing

OEM means the buyer provides the product concept, design, or requirements.

The factory manufactures according to those specifications.

Suitable for brands that want:

- Unique products
- Full control
- Custom designs

---

## ODM Manufacturing

ODM means the factory provides existing product solutions that can be customized.

Suitable for companies that want:

- Faster product launch
- Lower development costs
- Proven product designs

Many new brands start with ODM and gradually move toward deeper OEM cooperation.
